# Delegated Authority

## 33.1 Purpose and Scope
Set out the powers retained by Shareholders, the Chair, Directors, and the Board, and powers delegated to the Managing Director, Executive Leadership Team and their teams.

## 33.2 Limitation
This Policy does not set out the delegated authority within Management.

### Interpretation

#### Companies Act
The Company, the Board, each Director, and each Shareholder have the rights, powers, duties, and obligations set out in the Act except to the extent that they are negated or modified, in accordance with the Act, by the Shareholders Deed, and Constitution.

#### Shareholders Deed
If there is any inconsistency or conflict between the terms of this Policy and the Shareholders Deed, the terms of this Shareholders Deed will prevail.

#### Constitution
If there is any inconsistency or conflict between the terms of this Policy and the terms of any Constitution, the terms of the Constitution will prevail.

#### Board Charter
If there is any inconsistency or conflict between the terms of this Policy and the terms of any Board Charter, the terms of the Board Charter will prevail.

### Powers retained by each Major Shareholder:
1. Ability to appoint, remove, and replace one Director to the Board of each Company.

2. Ability to transfer or otherwise dispose of legal or beneficial ownership, or control of any Shares to the Related Principal, a trust, or a company with a Controlling Interest.

### Powers retained by Major Shareholders with Simple Majority:
3. Ability to appoint all other Directors to the Board of each Company.

4. Ability to appoint the Chair of the Board of each Company.

5. Ability to approve creating a Security Interest, except in the ordinary course of business for ongoing supply of stock, materials, or labour.

6. Ability to approve borrowing money or raising financial accommodation not in the ordinary course of business or leading to external indebtedness of $100,000.00 or more.

7. Ability to approve giving guarantees or indemnities by the Company, Shareholder, or Director.

8. Ability to approve the sale, transfer, or lease of assets exceeding $250,000.00.

9. Ability to approve entering agreements regarding real property exceeding $250,000 per annum.

10. Ability to approve any Shareholder, Scheme Shareholder or Principals involvement in a competing business activity.

### Powers retained by each Scheme Shareholder:
11. Ability to transfer or dispose of beneficial ownership of its Shares to a Related Principal or a trust/company with a Controlling Interest.

### Powers retained by Directors appointed by Founding Shareholders (agreeing unanimously):
12. Ability to approve Shareholder transfer of Shares to family members.

### Powers retained by Directors appointed by Major Shareholders (agreeing unanimously):
13. Ability to require the repayment of Shareholder Loan(s).

14. Ability to approve the requirement for Major Shareholders' to provide guarantees, indemnities, or commitments to support the obligations of any Company.

### Powers retained by each Director:
15. Ability to appoint an alternate Director (once that alternate Director has been approved by the Board).

16. Ability to convene a Board meeting.

17. Ability to request agenda item additions.

18. Ability to access Company information and Management.

19. Ability to obtain independent professional advice.

### Powers retained by the Chair:
20. Ability to call for votes when consensus is not reached.

21. Approving the agenda for Board meetings.

22. Representing the Board to Shareholders.

23. Ensuring governance process integrity is upheld at all times.

24. Facilitating Board meetings.

25. Approving reimbursement for Director's professional advice.

### Powers retained by the Board:
26. Determining fair value for Share sell down or transfer.

27. Approving or amending the Annual Operating Plan and Budget.

28. Approving surety fees payable to Major Shareholders.

29. Approving the distribution of net profits to Shareholders.

30. Ability to issue shares to other persons.

31. Approving transfer of Scheme Shareholders' beneficial ownership.

32. Granting Security Interest over Shares.

33. Approving Accession Deed terms.

34. Issuing Shares (and options) as deemed fit.

35. Declining Share transfer registration.

36. Calling Shareholders for unpaid amounts.

37. Deducting unpaid amounts from dividends.

38. Determining interest rate on unpaid shares.

39. Authorizing Director remuneration, benefits, loans, guarantees, and compensations.

40. Approving alternative Directors.

41. Determining terms of indemnity for liability or costs.

42. Approving insurance for Directors and/or employees.

43. Approving signatories on behalf of the Company.

44. Electing a Temporary Chair for a Board Meeting should the incumbent be absent and not able to Chair the meeting remotely.

45. Ensuring Company Strategy, policies, compliance, and ethical standards are established.

46. Taking necessary steps to protect the financial position and meet obligations.

47. Ensuring true and fair financial statements, shareholder communications, annual reports, health, and safety compliance, and evaluating Board performance.

48. Appointing the Board Secretary.

49. Agreeing to sub-delegation levels immediately below the Managing Director.

### Board Delegates:
50. Day-to-day management delegated to Management.

51. Board authority to Management conveyed through the Managing Director.

52. Managing Director represents the Board to stakeholders.

53. Signing of Warranties in the standard form of; Schedule 13 of NZS3910:2013; Schedule 13 of the Underlying Contract as agreed to in the Directory of Construction Suppliers for Major Works with the Secretary of Education and The Masterspec section 1237WA Warranty Agreement, as per the standing resolution dated 26/07/2024.

### Approved sub-delegation to Executive Leadership Team:
54. Entering into employment or consulting/contracting agreements for budgeted positions.

55. Varying the terms of employment or consulting/contracting agreements (hours, salary, benefits, etc).

However, for items 54. and 55. countersign by Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO) is required. In the case of the CHRO, countersign by the Managing Director is required.

56. Overhead expenses up to budget.

However, for item 56. countersign by Chief Commercial Officer (CCO) is required for capital items over budget and/or greater than $3k (excl GST), or to incur expenses that extend beyond the current financial year (e.g. leases and recurring software licences).

### Approved sub-delegation to CCO:
57. Setting up, amending, and closing bank accounts.

58. Intercompany loans.

However, for items 57. and 58. countersign by the Managing Director is required.

### Approved sub-delegation to Chief Operating Officer (COO):
58. Decision to bid for Contracts.

59. Entering into Contracts, or varying existing Contracts, to provide goods and services.

60. Approving the level of Damages for Late Completion up to 30% of the declared amount for off-site overheads and profit.

61. Approving the form and value of the Contractor's Bond, if any.

62. Approving the form and value of Security for Payment.

63. Approving the type, level, and deductibles for insurance.

64. Agreeing to supplier terms and conditions.

However, for items 61. – 64. Countersign by the CCO is required.

65. Entering into Contracts to acquire goods and services for the purpose of delivering the Contract Works.

However, for item 65. countersign by the COO is required for capital items over budget and/or greater than $3k (excl GST) or to incur expenses that extend beyond the contract duration.

66. Decision to issue a Notice of mediation, arbitration, frustration or default.

68. Overhead expenses up to budget.

### Approved sub-delegation to other roles: Commercial Managers
