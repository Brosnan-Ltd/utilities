# Discrimination, Harrassment and Workplace Bullying

## 16.1 Purpose
Brosnan is committed to providing a work environment that is safe, respectful and free from discrimination, harassment (including sexual harassment) and bullying for all employees, contractors, clients and visitors.

This policy sets out the standards of behaviour expected of everyone who works at or visits Brosnan sites and offices, and the process for dealing with complaints.

## 16.2 Scope
This policy applies to all employees (permanent, fixed-term and casual), contractors, subcontractors, volunteers, interns, apprentices and anyone else working for or visiting Brosnan.

Behaviour that occurs:
- At work (office or site)
- At work-related events (e.g. social functions, conferences, Christmas parties)
- Between people who work together outside normal work hours (e.g. via phone, text, email or social media)
is covered by this policy.

## 16.3 Our Commitment
Brosnan does not tolerate discrimination, harassment or bullying under any circumstances. Any breach will be treated seriously and may result in disciplinary action up to and including immediate dismissal.

We will:
- Treat all complaints seriously and confidentially
- Act promptly to investigate and resolve complaints
- Protect complainants and witnesses from victimisation or retaliation
- Provide support to anyone affected

## 16.4 Definitions

### Discrimination
Treating someone less favourably because of a protected characteristic under the Human Rights Act 1993, including:
- Sex (including pregnancy and childbirth)
- Marital or family status
- Religious or ethical belief
- Colour, race, ethnic or national origins
- Disability
- Age
- Political opinion
- Employment status
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity

### Harassment (including Sexual Harassment)
Unwanted behaviour that:
- Offends, humiliates or intimidates another person
- Is based on a protected characteristic
- Creates a hostile or intimidating work environment

Sexual harassment includes:
- Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favours
- Leering, suggestive comments or jokes
- Unwanted physical contact
- Displaying offensive material
- Sending sexually explicit messages or images

### Bullying
Repeated and unreasonable behaviour directed towards a worker or group of workers that creates a risk to health and safety. Examples include:
- Verbal abuse, shouting or swearing
- Constant criticism or belittling
- Spreading malicious rumours
- Excluding or isolating someone
- Giving impossible deadlines or excessive workloads
- Practical jokes or initiation rituals that cause distress

Note: Legitimate performance management, reasonable instructions and fair disciplinary action are NOT bullying.

## 16.5 Responsibilities

### All Employees and Contractors
- Treat others with respect and courtesy
- Not engage in discrimination, harassment or bullying
- Report any behaviour that breaches this policy
- Cooperate with investigations

### Managers and Supervisors
- Model appropriate behaviour
- Take immediate action when made aware of inappropriate behaviour
- Support anyone who raises a concern
- Maintain confidentiality

## 16.7 Investigation Process
All complaints will be:
- Taken seriously
- Investigated promptly, fairly and confidentially
- Documented
- Free from bias

Both the complainant and respondent will have the opportunity to respond.

Possible outcomes include:
- No further action (if unsubstantiated)
- Mediation or conciliation
- Training or counselling
- Formal warnings or disciplinary action
- Termination of employment/contract

## 16.8 Malicious or False Complaints
Making a deliberately false or malicious complaint is serious misconduct and may result in disciplinary action.

## 16.9 Victimisation
Any retaliation against someone who has made a complaint or participated in an investigation is prohibited and will be treated as serious misconduct.

## 16.10 Confidentiality
Only those who need to know will be informed about the complaint.

## 16.11 External Options
If you are not satisfied with the internal process, you may contact:
- Employment New Zealand
- The Human Rights Commission
- WorkSafe (for health and safety-related bullying)

## 16.12 Review
This policy will be reviewed regularly to ensure it remains effective and compliant with legislation.

All employees will receive training on this policy as part of induction and ongoing workplace training.

Any questions about this policy should be directed to your manager or HR.
