# Vehicle

## 22.1 Purpose
To ensure all company vehicles are operated safely, maintained properly, used only for authorised purposes, and remain in good condition while protecting the Companyâ€™s reputation and minimising risk.

## 22.2 Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and any other persons who are provided with or drive a Brosnan-owned, leased, or rented vehicle (including utes, vans, and cars).

## 22.3 Vehicle Categories

| Category                  | Eligibility & Conditions                                                                 |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Full-time allocated vehicle | Generally provided only to senior management, project managers, and certain roles that require regular travel. Remains with the employee for work and limited private use. |
| Pool vehicles             | Available for booking and work use only. No private use unless pre-approved by a manager. |
| Site utes / vans          | Primarily for site-to-site travel and transporting tools/materials. Private use strictly prohibited unless authorised. |

## 22.4 Private Use of Company Vehicles
- Private use is a privilege, not a right, and is only permitted where expressly stated in your employment agreement or approved in writing.
- Private use is limited to the employee and immediate family members living at the same address.
- Private use must comply with all road rules and this policy at all times.
- All private use attracts Fringe Benefit Tax (FBT), which is paid by the Company.

## 22.5 Driver Responsibilities
Every driver of a company vehicle must:
- Hold a current, valid New Zealand driver licence appropriate for the vehicle
- Immediately report any licence suspension, disqualification, or serious infringement
- Complete a daily pre-start vehicle check (tyres, lights, fluids, damage)
- Report all accidents, damage, or faults immediately (no matter how minor)
- Keep the vehicle clean, tidy, and free of rubbish
- Never drive under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or any substance that impairs driving
- Always wear a seatbelt and ensure passengers do the same
- Not use a mobile phone (hand-held) while driving
- Secure all loads correctly and within legal weight limits
- Park legally and securely at all times

## 22.6 Prohibited Activities
- Carrying unauthorised passengers
- Towing without prior approval and correct licence
- Modifying or adding accessories without approval
- Smoking or vaping in any company vehicle
- Transporting pets (unless in an approved cage in a ute tray)
- Using the vehicle for racing, rallies, or any competitive event

## 22.7 Accidents and Damage
- Stop immediately, ensure safety, and call emergency services if required
- Exchange details and take photos
- Notify your manager and complete an Incident Report within 24 hours
- Do not admit liability

## 22.8 Fuel and Charging
- Company fuel card or charge card to be used only for the allocated vehicle
- No purchasing of fuel/charging for private vehicles or other people
- Misuse of fuel/charge cards will result in disciplinary action and repayment

## 22.9 Maintenance and Servicing
- All servicing is scheduled and arranged by the office
- Drivers must present the vehicle on the due date
- Drivers are responsible for keeping the vehicle clean (interior and exterior)

## 22.10 Loss of Driving Privileges or Vehicle
Driving privileges or use of a company vehicle may be withdrawn at any time for:
- Serious or repeated breaches of this policy
- Loss of licence
- Poor driving record
- Change in role requirements

## 22.11 End of Employment
All company vehicles, keys, fuel cards, and toll tags must be returned on or before the last day of employment. Final pay may be withheld until everything is returned.

Any questions about vehicles should be directed to your manager or the office administrator.
