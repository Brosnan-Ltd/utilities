# Health insurance and Employee Assistance Programme

## 18.1 Purpose
Brosnan recognises the importance of supporting the health and wellbeing of its employees. To help achieve this, the Company provides access to health insurance and a confidential Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) for all permanent full-time and part-time employees.

## 18.2 Health Insurance

### Eligibility
- All permanent employees working 20 hours or more per week are eligible after completing 3 months of continuous service.
- Cover commences on the first day of the month following the 3-month qualifying period.

### Coverage
- The Company pays 50% of the base premium for the employee only (single cover).
- Employees may choose to add partners and/or dependent children at their own cost (100% employee-funded).
- The level of cover is the standard Southern Cross Wellbeing 1 plan (or equivalent if the provider changes).
- Optional extras (e.g. dental, optical) can be added at the employee’s own expense.

### Key Features (Wellbeing 1 – current as at 2025)
- GP visits
- Prescriptions
- Specialist consultations
- Diagnostic tests
- Physiotherapy, chiropractic, osteopathy
- Hospitalisation and surgical benefits
- Cancer treatment support

Full details of the current plan are available on the Southern Cross member portal or from HR.

### Leaving the Company
- On termination of employment, cover ceases at the end of the month in which employment ends.
- Employees may choose to continue the policy privately through Southern Cross.

## 18.3 Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)

### Provider
Brosnan provides access to a fully confidential, professional counselling service through Vitae (or the current provider).

### What it Covers
- Personal and work-related issues
- Stress, anxiety, depression
- Relationship or family problems
- Grief and loss
- Financial or legal concerns (initial advice)
- Addiction issues
- Work-related conflict

### Access
- Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
- Phone: 0508 664 981
- Up to 5 free sessions per issue per year for the employee and immediate family members living at the same address
- Completely confidential – the Company is not informed of who uses the service or the nature of the issue

## 18.4 How to Join / Access

### Health Insurance
- New eligible employees will be contacted by HR near the end of their 3-month qualifying period.
- Enrolment forms must be completed and returned before the deadline to ensure continuous cover.

### EAP
- No enrolment required – simply call the 0508 number anytime.
- Information posters and wallet cards are displayed in offices and on sites.

## 18.5 Changes to Benefits
The Company reserves the right to change providers or benefit levels with reasonable notice. Employees will be advised of any significant changes.

Any questions about health insurance or the EAP should be directed to HR in confidence.
