# Media and Public Relations

## 35.1	Purpose and Scope
To ensure the Company’s external communication activity is fit for purpose and supports the Company’s vision, values, and objectives. This policy applies to all media and public relations activities.

## 35.2	Definitions

### Staff
This comprises Company employees and other individuals carrying out paid or unpaid work for the Company.

### External Stakeholders
This includes, but is not limited to, media, clients, consultants, subcontractors, suppliers, community groups and the general public.

### Communications Activity

This covers a broad range of activities, comprising but not limited to:
- Media releases, interviews, and written interview statements
- Published material, including articles, reports, and brochures
- Interviews, speeches, and award submissions
- Branding at a project site
- Photography and videography
- Digital and social media
- Advertising

## 35.3	Communication Requests
All Communication Activity requests by Staff and external stakeholders are to be directed to the CEO.

## 35.4	Media Requests

The CEO is the approved spokesperson for the Company and is responsible for liaising with the media.

## 35.5	Client Confidentiality

When publicity restrictions are in effect on a project, Staff must adhere to stated requirements at all times.       To not do so may be considered serious misconduct.

## 35.6	Staff Confidentiality

Staff are not permitted to engage in Communications Activity information about the Company either during employment or on termination of employment. 

This includes but is not limited to:
- Information that may bring the Company into disrepute
- Use of, or disclosure of, Company property such as logos, photos or other identifiable images, or any other Company information confidential or otherwise

Derogatory/offensive/unauthorised comments about the Company, other employees, projects, clients, subcontractors, or suppliers.
