# Social Media

## 41.1 Purpose and Scope

The Company respects the rights of individuals to use social media. This policy does not intend to discourage or unreasonably limit employees’ freedom of expression or online activities. However, employees (and other workplace participants, for example, contractors) are expected to be respectful in their communication and adhere to this policy (and relevant provisions in any other policies) at all times when using social media about their work and workplace, in that references to the Company in social media may have the potential to affect its reputation and public perception and therefore its relationships with employees, customers (actual and potential), suppliers, business partners or any other parties associated with the Company (including the public).

41.1.1
: The purpose of this policy is to ensure that employees (and other workplace participants) are aware of the Company’s expectations and requirements regarding the appropriate use of social media in their work and workplace.

41.1.2
: This policy applies to all employees and other persons engaged by the Company from time to time (for example contractors, referred to in this policy as “others”) about the use of social media, irrespective of whether use occurs during work or outside of work and/or on private devices.

41.1.3
: The Company reserves the right of approval for social media content related to its business. The Company is entitled to monitor the content and use of its social media channels.

41.1.4
: This policy must be read in conjunction with other policies, including but not limited to the Company’s disciplinary and/or discrimination, harassment, and workplace bullying policies.

## 41.2 Definitions

41.2.1
: For this policy, the term social media includes but is not limited to:
a.social networking sites (for example, Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok, Snapchat)
b. video and photo sharing websites (for example, YouTube, Instagram, Flickr)
c. blogs, Twitter, forums, discussion boards and groups (for example, Google groups, LinkedIn)
d. online encyclopaedias (for example, Wikipedia)
e. instant messaging (including chat and SMS applications such as Microsoft Teams)
f. any other website or web-based tool and any other emerging electronic or digital communications applications.

## 41.3 Employee Responsibilities

41.3.1
: All employees (and others) must read and comply with this policy.

41.3.2
: All employees (and others) are expected to promote and be respectful of the interests and reputation of the Company, its employees, suppliers, customers, business partners or any other parties associated with the Company’s business in all communications when making comments or publishing material on the internet, including social media.

41.3.3
: The Company prohibits and considers to constitute serious misconduct, which may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal, the posting of comments, sending of messages or publishing of materials on the internet (including social media), if this:

41.3.4
: Breaches the privacy rights of an employee or any other person associated with the Company in any way (for example, posting photos or other personal information without the relevant persons’ consent)

41.3.5
: Constitutes any kind of harassment, discrimination or bullying of an employee or any other person associated with the Company

41.3.6
: Is offensive, obscene, inflammatory, threatening, racist, sexist, victimising, or otherwise inappropriate comments in the circumstances

41.3.7
: Has the potential to adversely affect the reputation of the Company, its employees, suppliers, customers, business partners or any other party associated with the Company

41.3.8
: Involves the use or disclosure of information that is confidential to the Company or can be reasonably considered to be commercially sensitive

41.3.9
: May be inconsistent with the Harmful Digital Communications Act 2015

41.3.10
: If an employee makes comments or publishes material on the internet, including any comments posted on social media, which the Company reasonably considers to be in breach of this policy, the employee may be instructed to immediately remove the comment or material from the internet/social media site. Any failure to abide by such instruction may constitute serious misconduct and may result in disciplinary action up to and including summary dismissal.

## 41.4 Professional Use of Social Media

41.4.1
: The creation and running of any internet/social media presence of the Company and on behalf of the Company is at all times subject to the Company’s approval.

41.4.2
: Where the Company uses social media for its business-related purposes, only expressly delegated/authorised persons (for example, the Communication, Brand and Marketing Manager), are permitted to create and publish Company-related content on social media.

41.4.3
: Employees who wish to contribute to the Company’s ‘official’ social media activities and accounts (for example, by providing potential content) must liaise with the delegated/authorised persons before being able to share, upload or publish Company-related content on any Company’s social media account. Prior approval must be obtained before posting/uploading:
    - any commentary or material can be made/published on behalf of the Company
    - reproducing emblems or logos owned by the Company
    - any product or service of the Company is promoted, supported, or endorsed; or
    - before employees must hold themselves out to be representative of the Company

41.4.4
: The form and content of any commentary or materials published on the internet/social media on behalf of the Company must be authorised by the Company.

41.4.5
: At no time must confidential or commercially sensitive information or intellectual property owned or controlled by the Company be posted on the internet/social media unless this has been expressly authorised.

41.4.6
: The posting of personal information of others (for example, photos and personal details) on Company social media/internet channels will require prior permission from the person’s such information relates to.

41.4.7
: Any publication of commentary or material on the internet/social media on behalf of the Company must comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

## 41.5 Personal Use of Social Media

41.5.1
: The Company acknowledges that it cannot direct or restrict the personal use of social media, etc. by its employees, particularly in their own time. However, Employee’s personal use of social media must align with the expectations and requirements referred to throughout this policy, as far as such use is or can be perceived as being related to their work and/or the Company, or where it involves the use of the Company’s IT equipment.

41.5.1
: Employees are solely responsible for any content they publish on the internet/social media in a personal capacity, regardless of whether or not such content directly or indirectly relates to the Company, unless the Company has expressly authorised such content before posting.

41.5.1
: Whilst a reasonable amount of social media use during work time is acceptable to the company, this must not interfere with the Employee’s employment duties or obligations and must not be illegal or contrary to the interests of the Company. The Employee shall also comply with all email and social media and internet policies issued by the Company from time to time.
