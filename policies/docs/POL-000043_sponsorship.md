# Sponsorship

## 44.1 Definition

Sponsorship is an arrangement between the Company and another organisation, or an individual, in exchange for money, goods, or services in return for opportunities to promote and enhance the Company's brand and to support the activities of the Company.

## 44.2 Purpose of Policy

To establish principles that must be consistently applied when assessing and approving corporate sponsorship requests.

## 44.3 Policy Objectives

The Company views its sponsorship contributions as investments that reflect its commitment to market relevant customers, communities, and partners. The policy objectives are to:

- Define what corporate sponsorship means.
- Understand the intent and objectives of Company sponsorships.
- Ensure that the Company enters into suitable sponsorship activities.
- Ensure sponsorships have measurable outcomes.
- Coordinate the Company's sponsorship activities.
- Help staff assess sponsorship proposals fairly and equitably.
- Outline ownership responsibilities.
- Promote understanding of the value of sponsorships.
- Ensure the Company maximises the full potential benefit from any sponsorship arrangement.

## 44.4 Policy Intent and Limits

The Company seeks to sponsor activities that promote and enhance the Company's reputation and brand via corporate sponsorship opportunities that are linked to the vertical building industry, notably within but not limited to, the Body Corporate and Property management sectors. Priority will be given to sponsorships where there is:

- A direct connection with the Company’s current and potential customers and associated stakeholders.
- An emphasis on education, upskilling and continuous improvement, whether of the customer and/or of Company employees.

Using the Sponsorship Submission Form, sponsorships will be reviewed and approved or declined by the CEO at the end of each calendar year for inclusion in the next financial year’s Annual Operating Plan (AOP).

## 44.5 Protocols, Responsibilities and Conditions

- The CEO must approve all sponsorships before any agreement is finalised and signed.
- The Company Business Unit involved must have approved agreement terms for all sponsorship arrangements and must require activities to be regularly reviewed and measured.
- Sponsorship agreements must have clear ownership and management responsibilities.
- The aggregate value of all sponsorships must fall within the Company's annual sponsorships budget.
- The GST treatment of any sponsorship must be determined and explicitly stated in the sponsorship agreement.
- In the negotiation of any sponsorship arrangement, the commercial value of any in-kind support must be identified and acknowledged by the agreeing parties.
- All new and renewed sponsorship agreements must be held in a central repository by Marketing.
- Sponsorship does not give either party to the agreement the right to represent or be a spokesperson for the other party.

## 44.6 Assessment Criteria

Sponsorships must be assessed against all the following criteria:

- Alignment with the Company's purpose, vision, mission, values, and policies
    - For more details, refer to Company Overview, notably the "we believe in" statements under "Our Purpose" and "Our Vision".
- Compatible with the Company's strategic goals and objectives.
- Create a connection and an opportunity for engagement and participation amongst the Company's prospective audience, including but not limited to prospective and current employees, customers, consultants, subcontractors, suppliers, educators, and industry advocacy organisations.
- It will positively impact the Company's visibility, market reputation, and brand, including the generation of new Prospects, Leads and relationships with relevant stakeholders within the target market.
- Allows publication of the Company's logos, as approved by Marketing.
- It is not linked to a registered political party, activist organisation, or group.
- It will benefit an organisation and not a person.
- It will not adversely affect the Company's autonomy or freedom.
- Does not compromise ethical standards.
- Does not obstruct or limit scrutiny.
- It is not illegal
